After several positions in the Electricity sector, Hubert Lemmens joined in 2003 the Elia Management Board (Comit de Direction), where he was successively Director of System Operation, Maintenance and Research and Innovation. He was deeply involved in the unbundling process and the carve-out of the Belgian System operator Elia from Electrabel from 1999 on.

Since 2006, Hubert Lemmens acted as coordinator of the European Wind Integration Study, a first of this kind study supported by the European Commission. He contributed to the Strategic Energy Technology Plan (SET Plan) of the European Commission as the driving force of the European Electricity Grid Initiative (EEGI).

Hubert chaired the ENTSO-E R&D committee for 3 years and led the development of the first R&D Roadmap of ENTSO-E.

Since 2011 hes representing the Elia Group in GO15, the association of the 18 largest power grid operators of the world. Hes actually co-chairing the Committee Grid for Flexible Resources.

In 2014 Hubert resigned from the Elia Management Board and became active as Senior Advisor in Elia. Hes also member of the Executive Board of the European Association for Storage of Energy.

 

In the course of his career Hubert build a broad experience in Power economics. Trough his international contacts with European and global power companies, he had the opportunity to get familiarized with different business models and organisations in the power sector. As member of Elias Comit de Direction he was deeply involved in the management of the company.
